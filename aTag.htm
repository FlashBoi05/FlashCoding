The < a > tag defines a hyperlink, which is used to link from
one page to another. The most important attribute of the < a > element is the href
attribute, which indicates the link's destination.

By default, links will appear as follows in all browsers:

(i)An unvisited link is underlined and blue
(ii)A visited link is underlined and purple
(iii)An active link is underlined and red


Example:


1)< a href=" CodingTutorial.com ">CodingTutorial< /a >





2)< a href="https://www.CodingTutorials.com">





3)< img border="0" alt="Coding" src="logo_Codes.gif" width="100" height="100"> < /a>





4)< a href="https://www.CodingTutorial.com" target="_blank">Visit CodingTutorial.com!< /a>





5)< a href="mailto:someone@example.com">Send email< /a>





6)< a href="tel:+4113561621">+41 423 45 502< /a>





7)< a href="#CodingTutorial">Go to CodingTutorial< /a>





8)< a href="javascript:alert('Hello World!');">Execute JavaScript< /a>
